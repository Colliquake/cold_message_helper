According to ChatGPT, for high-success approach:
"The key is that the conversation has to feel genuine rather than like you're
delaying the referral request by a few messages.

A good progression is:

1. Reference something specific about them.
2. Ask one thoughtful question.
3. Ask if they'd be open to a brief chat (or just continue over LinkedIn if they prefer).
4. Have a real conversation.
5. If the conversation goes well, ask for the referral."

Then, during the chat:
"Spend most of the time learning about them:
- 'What made you join Company X?'
- 'What's surprised you most about working there?'
- 'How is the engineering culture?'
- What kinds of projects do junior engineers usually work on?'
etc."

Then, near the end, if the conversation has gone well, say that you're even more excited.
Something like:
"Thanks-- this has been really helpful. Based on what you've shared, I'm even more interested
in applying. If you think my background is a good fit, would you be comfortable referring me?
No worries at all if not."





Best days and times to message people:
Tuesday - Thursday tend to be the strongest days.
(people are mentally checked out on Fridays, weekends generally have lower response rates,
and mondays are often spent catching up on work and meetings)
